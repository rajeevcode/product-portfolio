The Problem: Scaling Travel Supply Without Breaking Booking Reliability or Pricing Accuracy

HafH needed to expand property supply across many regions while maintaining booking reliability, pricing accuracy, partner readiness, and operational visibility. The challenge was not just adding more hotels; it was creating the platform workflows, data mapping, pricing validation, and operational feedback loops needed to scale reliably.

Limited property supply scale

A relatively small supply base capped the value of the subscription for travelers across regions.

Partner onboarding friction

Bringing new hotel partners online was slow and inconsistent, with no structured readiness path.

Inconsistent property data mapping

Property attributes, rates, and availability arrived in different shapes from different partners.

Booking confirmation reliability gaps

Confirmation outcomes varied, undermining traveler trust at the moment of purchase.

Pricing accuracy variation by region

Pricing drifted across markets, creating mismatches between displayed and bookable rates.

Manual production issue triage

Integration and availability issues were caught reactively, one ticket at a time.

Limited partner performance visibility

Teams lacked a clear view of which partners and regions were healthy or at risk.

Regional business rules complexity

Each market carried its own rules, making consistent platform behavior hard to guarantee.

Solution / product approach

A layered flow turning raw partner supply into scalable, reliable travel platform growth.

  1. Partner supply / property data / regional rules, ingest inputs from partners and markets.
  2. ↓ API integration & data mapping, normalize property, rate, and availability data.
  3. ↓ Partner onboarding workflow, structured readiness path for new partners.
  4. ↓ Availability & booking reliability checks, validate before exposure to travelers.
  5. ↓ ML-assisted pricing validation, catch and correct pricing drift.
  6. ↓ Operational monitoring & triage, detect and resolve issues proactively.
  7. ↓ Scalable travel platform growth, reliable supply expansion as the outcome.

Product manager takeaways

Scale is a reliability problem

Adding supply is easy; keeping confirmations and pricing trustworthy at scale is the real product work.

Balancing business and tech

I tied supply-growth ambitions to concrete API reliability, fallback, and validation requirements.

Handling ambiguity

Heterogeneous partner data and regional rules meant designing for variance, not a single happy path.

Measuring success

Tracked supply, booking reliability, and pricing accuracy together so growth never outran quality.

My operating style

Move from reactive triage to proactive monitoring and structured onboarding workflows.
