The Problem: B2B Commerce, Payments, and Finance Operations Were Fragmented Across Teams and Tools

Mumzworld/Tamer needed a scalable B2B platform that could connect partner onboarding, payments, approvals, finance workflows, reconciliation, ERP/CRM data, and operating dashboards. The business challenge was reducing manual work while creating better visibility, governance, and partner workflows.

Fragmented partner onboarding

Bringing B2B partners on board was inconsistent and spread across disconnected steps.

Manual finance workflows

Finance operations relied on manual effort across multiple tools.

Slow reconciliation

Reconciling payments and ledgers was slow and error-prone.

Payment visibility gaps

Teams lacked a clear, shared view of payment status and flows.

Approval bottlenecks

Approvals lacked structured rules, creating delays and inconsistency.

ERP/CRM integration complexity

Connecting ERP, CRM, and payment data was technically and operationally complex.

High operating cost

Manual, fragmented workflows drove avoidable operating cost.

Limited leadership dashboards

Leadership lacked reliable, consolidated operating visibility.

Product scope

In scope

  • B2B partner onboarding and commerce workflows
  • Payment workflows and reconciliation logic
  • ERP (NetSuite) and CRM (HubSpot) integrations
  • Approval & control rules and finance automation
  • Operating dashboards and reporting

Out of scope

  • Consumer-facing e-commerce storefront redesign
  • Banking / payment processor infrastructure ownership
  • Company-wide ERP migration program
  • Pricing and commercial strategy ownership

Solution / product approach

A layered flow turning fragmented inputs into an integrated B2B commerce and payments platform.

  1. Partner / commercial / finance inputs, capture needs from across functions.
  2. ↓ B2B onboarding flow, structured partner activation.
  3. ↓ ERP + CRM + payment data integration, one connected data backbone.
  4. ↓ Approval & control rules, governance built into the workflow.
  5. ↓ Finance / support / reconciliation automation, reduce manual effort.
  6. ↓ Dashboards & partner workflows, shared visibility and self-service.
  7. ↓ Revenue, cost, and operating impact, ARR, cost reduction, faster processing.

Product manager takeaways

0-to-1 is about sequencing

Shipping a platform meant ruthless sequencing of integrations, payments, and governance against business value.

Balancing business and tech

I translated ARR and cost goals into concrete ERP/CRM, payment, and control-rule requirements.

Handling ambiguity

Seven functions with different needs required clear scope and governance, not a single owner's wishlist.

Measuring success

Tracked ARR, operating cost, and processing time together so the platform proved its value.

My operating style

Embed governance and automation into workflows so scale doesn't reintroduce manual effort.
